How We Evaluated AI Meeting Assistants

Our testing methodology focused on real-world performance, not marketing claims. We evaluated each tool across five critical dimensions:

Testing Methodology

1. Transcription Accuracy (Word Error Rate) We measured WER in two environments: - Clean audio: Controlled office setting, quality microphone, minimal background noise - Noisy audio: Coffee shop ambiance, multiple speakers, HVAC noise, keyboard typing

Many tools advertise "95% accuracy" but only achieve this in perfect conditions. Our noisy audio tests revealed which tools maintain performance when meetings get messy.

2. Speaker Diarization Accuracy Can the tool correctly identify who said what? This matters enormously for multi-person meetings, especially when creating action items assigned to specific team members.

3. Action Item Extraction Precision We counted how accurately each tool identified genuine action items versus hallucinating tasks that were never discussed or missing critical commitments.

4. Summary Latency Time from meeting end to usable summary delivery. In fast-paced environments, a 30-second turnaround versus 2+ minutes can impact decision velocity.

5. Bot vs. Bot-Free Models Does the tool join as a visible participant (bot) or capture audio invisibly (bot-free)? This impacts privacy, compliance, and meeting dynamics.

Our performance benchmarks come from Krisp.ai's comprehensive February 2026 testing, which evaluated 10 major platforms using standardized test scenarios. We've supplemented this with our own pricing research, user reviews from Reddit communities (r/NoteTaking, r/buhaydigital), and G2 ratings.

Bot vs. Bot-Free: Which Model Fits Your Needs?

One of the most important decisions when choosing an AI meeting assistant is the bot model. This fundamental architecture choice impacts privacy, compliance, meeting etiquette, and available features.

Bot Joiners (Virtual Participant Model)

How it works: A virtual participant (like "Otter.ai Notetaker" or "Fireflies.ai") joins your video call as a visible attendee. Audio is sent to the vendor's cloud servers for transcription.

Tools using this model: Otter.ai, Fireflies.ai, Fathom, Avoma, tl;dv, Fellow.ai, Sembly AI

Advantages: - Universal compatibility: Works with any video platform (Zoom, Teams, Meet, Webex) - Rich features: Real-time transcription, live reactions, in-meeting commands - Reliable capture: Less dependent on local device quality - No app installation: Works from browser or calendar integration

Disadvantages: - Visible to all participants: Creates "AI bot has joined" moment - Privacy concerns: Audio sent to vendor cloud for processing - Permission requirements: May require host approval in some settings - Meeting etiquette: Can feel awkward in client/prospect meetings - Compliance complexity: Data residency and retention must be reviewed

Bot-Free (On-Device or Browser Extension Model)

How it works: Audio is captured locally via desktop app, browser extension, or mobile app. Processing happens on-device or in minimal cloud interaction.

Tools using this model: Krisp, Granola, Jamie, HappyScribe (upload option), Equal Time

Advantages: - Invisible capture: No visible bot participant - Privacy-first: On-device processing minimizes cloud data transfer - Compliance-friendly: Easier GDPR, HIPAA, EU AI Act compliance - Meeting etiquette: No awkward "AI bot has joined" announcement - In-person support: Some tools work for in-person meetings

Disadvantages: - App installation required: Desktop/mobile app adds friction - Platform limitations: May not work with all video platforms - Limited real-time features: Harder to do live reactions, commands - Device dependency: Quality depends on local microphone - Feature maturity: Generally less feature-rich than bot competitors

Decision Framework: Which Model Suits You?

Choose Bot Joiners if: - You primarily have internal meetings with your team - You need rich features (real-time transcription, live collaboration) - Platform compatibility matters (Teams one day, Zoom the next) - Data processing in vendor cloud is acceptable - Your IT/compliance team approves cloud storage

Choose Bot-Free if: - You frequently meet with clients, prospects, or external parties - Privacy and compliance are top priorities (healthcare, legal, finance) - You're subject to GDPR, HIPAA, or EU AI Act requirements - You find bots intrusive or awkward - You need support for in-person meetings

Hybrid approach: Many teams use bot joiners (Fireflies, Otter) for internal meetings and bot-free tools (Krisp, Granola) for external/sensitive meetings. This requires managing two subscriptions but provides flexibility.

The EU AI Act Factor

The EU AI Act, which came into effect in 2024-2025, classifies AI systems by risk level. Meeting assistants with emotion recognition or biometric identification fall into "high-risk" categories requiring strict compliance.

Compliant by design: Krisp and HappyScribe have published EU AI Act compliance statements and operate with on-device processing or EU data centers.

Under review: Most bot-based tools are still evaluating their risk classification and compliance pathways.

Practical impact: European companies should prioritize vendors with published EU AI Act compliance statements to avoid procurement delays and legal risk.

2026 Trends Shaping AI Meeting Assistants

1. The EU AI Act Drives Bot-Free Adoption

The EU AI Act's risk-based classification system is pushing European companies toward bot-free, on-device processing tools. Vendors without clear compliance statements are being removed from procurement shortlists.

Impact: Expect Krisp, HappyScribe, and Granola to gain European market share while bot-based tools scramble to publish compliance roadmaps.

2. On-Device Processing Improves Privacy & Latency

Apple's Neural Engine, Qualcomm's Snapdragon AI, and Intel's AI Boost are enabling sophisticated on-device transcription without cloud dependencies. Krisp's approach (on-device noise cancellation, minimal cloud transcription) is becoming the blueprint.

Impact: By late 2026, expect on-device transcription quality to match cloud-based tools, eliminating the accuracy trade-off for privacy-focused tools.

3. Mobile-First Design for Hybrid Workers

As hybrid work normalizes, mobile apps are becoming primary interfaces, not afterthoughts. Tools with strong mobile experiences (Otter, Fireflies, Krisp) are gaining favor among traveling executives and field sales teams.

Impact: Desktop-first tools (Granola, some features of tl;dv) must invest in mobile parity or risk losing mobile-heavy user segments.

4. In-Person Meeting Support Expands

Jamie's success highlighting in-person meeting capture has competitors scrambling to add similar features. Expect Krisp, Otter, and Fireflies to announce in-person recording capabilities in 2026.

Impact: The distinction between "video call transcription" and "meeting assistant" will blur as tools support any meeting format.

5. Audio Quality Becomes Table Stakes

Krisp's noise cancellation success has shown that transcription accuracy depends heavily on audio quality. Zoom, Teams, and Meet have all announced enhanced AI noise cancellation for 2026.

Impact: Built-in platform noise cancellation may reduce the need for separate Krisp subscriptions, but specialized tools will maintain an edge for noisy environments.

6. Consolidation and Platform Integration

Zoom's AI Companion, Microsoft 365 Copilot, and Google's Duet AI are integrating meeting transcription directly into video platforms. This creates pressure on standalone tools to differentiate on features, not basic transcription.

Impact: Specialized tools (Fireflies for CRM automation, tl;dv for coaching, Granola for customization) will thrive; generic transcription-only tools face existential risk.

4. What's the difference between bot and bot-free meeting assistants?

Bot assistants (Otter, Fireflies, Fathom) join your meeting as a visible participant. This enables universal compatibility and rich features but raises privacy concerns and can feel awkward in client meetings.

Bot-free assistants (Krisp, Granola, Jamie) capture audio locally via desktop/mobile apps without appearing in the participant list. This prioritizes privacy and meeting etiquette but may limit features and platform compatibility.

Choose bot-free if: Privacy matters, you meet with clients/prospects frequently, or you're subject to GDPR/HIPAA regulations.

5. Do AI meeting assistants work for in-person meetings?

Jamie is specifically designed for in-person meetings, recording via desktop or mobile app. Krisp also supports in-person capture through its desktop app.

Most other tools (Otter, Fireflies, Fathom) require video conferencing platforms and won't work for in-person conference room meetings. This is a major gap that vendors are beginning to address in 2026.

Workaround: Record in-person meetings on your phone, then upload to HappyScribe or other tools with post-meeting upload capabilities.

6. Which AI meeting assistant is most private and secure?

Krisp leads on privacy with on-device processing, zero-day data retention, and EU AI Act compliance. HappyScribe follows closely with GDPR compliance, EU data centers, and user-controlled retention.

For HIPAA compliance: Fellow.ai and Krisp offer HIPAA Business Associate Agreements at accessible price points.

For minimal data retention: Krisp (0 days), Granola (30 days, transcript-only), Fellow.ai (90 days)
