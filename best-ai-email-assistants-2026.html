Privacy and Security Considerations

Before granting any AI email assistant access to your inbox, consider these privacy factors:

What Permissions Does It Request?

Most AI email tools require "read and write" access to your email. However, the scope varies:

  • Read-only for organization: Tools like SaneBox and Clean Email only need to read email metadata (sender, subject, date) to organize your inbox.
  • Full access for writing: Tools like Superhuman, Lindy, and MailMaestro need write access to send emails on your behalf.

Where Is Your Data Stored?

  • Cloud-processed: Most tools (Superhuman, Lindy, Shortwave) process emails on their servers. Look for privacy policies that commit to not selling your data.
  • On-device processing: Some tools process locally without sending email content to external servers.
  • Open source: Inbox Zero lets you audit the code and optionally self-host for complete control.

What About Email Content?

  • Anonymization: Tools like GPT for Gmail anonymize emails before processing.
  • No storage: Some tools claim not to store email content after processing.
  • Training prohibition: Look for explicit commitments not to use your emails to train AI models.

Red Flags to Watch For

  • Vague privacy policies without specific commitments
  • No mention of data retention policies
  • Lack of SOC 2 or similar security certifications
  • Inability to delete your data on request
  • Third-party data sharing without explicit consent

Best practices: - Review permissions carefully before connecting - Use app-specific passwords instead of main account credentials when possible - Regularly audit which apps have access to your email - Consider using AI email tools only for work accounts, keeping personal email separate

Frequently Asked Questions

Are AI email assistants safe to use?

Reputable AI email assistants use OAuth authentication (the same method banks and financial apps use) and encrypt data in transit and at rest. However, you are granting access to potentially sensitive information, so choose tools with strong privacy policies, security certifications (SOC 2), and explicit commitments not to sell your data or use it for AI training without consent.

Can AI email assistants replace my current email client?

Some AI email assistants like Superhuman and Shortwave are full email clients that replace Gmail or Outlook entirely. Others like SaneBox, MailMaestro, and GPT for Gmail work as add-ons to your existing email client. Choose based on whether you want a complete replacement or just enhanced functionality.

How accurate are AI-generated email drafts?

Accuracy varies by tool and improves over time. Tools like Superhuman and MailMaestro that learn from your sent emails produce more personalized drafts that match your voice. Generic tools like GPT for Gmail produce high-quality writing but may not sound like you without editing. Always review AI-generated drafts before sending, especially for sensitive or important communications.

Will AI email assistants work with my CRM?

Tools like Superhuman (Salesforce, HubSpot), Lindy (1,600+ apps), and Gmelius offer CRM integrations. Most writing-focused tools like MailMaestro and GPT for Gmail do not integrate with CRMs. If CRM integration is critical, prioritize tools that explicitly support your CRM platform.

Can I use AI email assistants on mobile?

Most AI email assistants offer mobile apps or mobile web access. Superhuman, Shortwave, and Gemini all work well on mobile. Tools that function as browser extensions (GPT for Gmail, MailMaestro) may have limited or no mobile support. Check each tool's mobile capabilities before committing if you frequently manage email on your phone.

Do AI email assistants support multiple email accounts?

Yes, but capabilities vary. Superhuman, Lindy, SaneBox, and Clean Email all support multiple email accounts. Some tools like Shortwave's Personal plan only support gmail.com addresses, while business plans support custom domains. Check multi-account support if you manage multiple inboxes.

What's the difference between email AI and email automation?

Email AI uses machine learning to understand context, generate natural language, and make intelligent decisions (like prioritizing important emails or drafting contextual replies). Email automation follows predefined rules you set up (like "move all emails from X sender to Y folder"). The best AI email assistants like Lindy and Superhuman combine both AI intelligence and rule-based automation.

Can AI email assistants handle attachments and file management?

Most AI email assistants can read and reference attachments in their summaries and suggestions. However, advanced attachment workflows (like automatically saving attachments to cloud storage or extracting data from PDFs) typically require automation-focused tools like Lindy or workflow platforms like Zapier in combination with your email AI.

Are there AI email assistants specifically for customer support?

Yes. Gmelius is purpose-built for support teams with shared inboxes, email assignment, and SLA tracking. Lindy also works well for support workflows with its automation capabilities. For general customer support AI (beyond email), explore dedicated helpdesk platforms with AI features.

What happens if I stop paying for an AI email assistant?

Most AI email assistants simply stop providing their AI features but don't affect your underlying email account. Your emails remain intact in Gmail, Outlook, or whatever provider you use. Tools like Superhuman that replace your email client will require you to switch back to your provider's native interface. Always export important data before canceling any subscription.
