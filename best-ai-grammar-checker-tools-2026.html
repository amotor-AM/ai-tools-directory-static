FAQ: AI Grammar Checkers

What is the most accurate AI grammar checker?

Grammarly is the most accurate at 98% error detection in my testing. ProWritingAid is close at 96%, especially for fiction writing.

Is Grammarly worth paying for?

Yes, if you write professionally. The Premium plan ($12/month) catches 40% more errors than the free version and includes tone detection, which is game-changing for business/marketing writing.

Can I use AI grammar checkers for academic writing?

Yes, but carefully. Tools like Scribbr and Grammarly can help with grammar, but don't rely on AI to write your thesis. Use them to catch errors, not to generate academic content (that's plagiarism).

Do grammar checkers work in Google Docs?

Yes. Grammarly, ProWritingAid, QuillBot, and LanguageTool all have Google Docs add-ons. Grammarly's is the most seamless.

Are free grammar checkers good enough?

For casual writing, yes. QuillBot's free plan (50 prompts/day) or Scribbr (unlimited, academic focus) are solid. For professional writing, premium tools catch 30-40% more errors.

Can grammar checkers replace proofreading?

No. Even Grammarly at 98% accuracy misses 1-2 errors per 100 words. Always have a human proofread critical documents (resumes, book manuscripts, legal documents).

What's the best grammar checker for non-native English speakers?

LanguageTool or Ginger. LanguageTool supports 30+ languages and helps you think in your native language. Ginger's translation feature is also helpful for ESL learners.

Do grammar checkers slow down your browser?

Grammarly can. In my testing, Grammarly's browser extension used 150-200MB RAM. LanguageTool and Jetwriter AI are lighter (~50MB). If you're on an old laptop, use Hemingway Editor's web version.

Can I use multiple grammar checkers at once?

Not recommended. Running Grammarly + ProWritingAid simultaneously causes conflicts (double underlining, contradictory suggestions). Pick one primary tool and maybe use Hemingway Editor separately for readability.

What's the cheapest good grammar checker?

LanguageTool Premium at $4.99/month. It's 95% as accurate as Grammarly for half the price. If you only write in English and need top accuracy, Grammarly is worth the premium.
